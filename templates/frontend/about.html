History

TSYDA was established in the year 2011 as an association of Toro indigenes and by extension Bauchi state. Its fundamental task as encapsulated in its motto youths for development was to build and sustain a viable socio-economic climate for the youths especially at the grassroot level. At inception, we took-off with few dozens of members, but before a year in existence the association blossomed with no fewer than a thousand active members. Today, it boasts of thousands and recieves scores of membership requests by the day.

We operate an open door policy, accomodating all manners of development-oriented people defying differences in religion, sex, accademic of financial status.

We have met all legal criteria for registration and got the blessings and support of our traditional and
and religious leaders.

We are non partisan in our operations, however, this does not prevent us from playing our role in political
and social processes for the benefit of all in the society.
